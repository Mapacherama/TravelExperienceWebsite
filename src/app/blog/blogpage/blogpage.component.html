Seeing the Brandenburger Tor

The Brandenburg Gate, or "Brandenburger Tor" as it's known locally, stands as a monumental testament to Berlin's rich tapestry of history. Erected in the 18th century, this neoclassical marvel has borne witness to the rise and fall of empires, the horrors of war, and the joyous celebrations of reunification. Dominated by the Quadriga, a chariot drawn by four horses atop its crown, the gate is a symbol of both division and unity.

The Challenges of My Mid-20s

Depressing showers

At the age of 24, the world outside my window seemed vast and unending, yet my own world felt painfully small. Every morning, as the first rays of sunlight peeked through, I'd already be reaching for my first drink of the day. By 8 AM, the sharp sting of alcohol would course through my veins, numbing the monotony of the life while I was taking the morning shower. I had unwittingly settled into a life I never wanted to be in.

Dead End Job

My days were defined by the repetitive rhythm of my footsteps as a postman, delivering letters to doorsteps that seemed to hold stories far more exciting than my own. The weight of the mailbag on my shoulder was nothing compared to the weight of the realization that I was trapped in a dead-end job, with no clear direction or purpose.

The escape

Evenings were no different. The digital landscapes of video games became my escape, a place where I could be a hero, even if it was just virtual. Alongside the immersive worlds of gaming, movies played a significant role in my escapism.

The cinematic tales, with their captivating narratives and characters, offered me a temporary respite from my own reality. They transported me to different worlds, times, and experiences, allowing me to live vicariously through the protagonists.

But once the credits rolled and the screen went dark, reality would set back in. I'd find myself aimlessly wandering the streets, lost in thought, wondering if this was all life had in store for me.

Becoming friends with the darkness

Strangely, there was a part of me that was drawn to this darkness. I found a perverse comfort in the depths of despair, almost craving the heavy blanket of depression. Drinking wasn't just an escape; it was a means to sink deeper into that abyss, to willingly drown in the melancholic waves that seemed to resonate with my soul.

Alongside this, I found myself drawn to truly vile and despicable True Crime podcasts. The darker and more depressing the content, the more I sought it out, as if trying to feed the insatiable beast of my own despair.

Stagnation and emptiness...

The vibrant energy of youth that should have propelled me forward was instead being squandered on aimless walks and the cold embrace of the bottle. It was a life of stagnation, where days blended into one another, marked only by the changing seasons and the growing sense of emptiness within.

Challenges to overcome:

Every day was a battle against overwhelming fatigue. The kind of tiredness that wasn't just physical but seeped deep into my bones, making every task feel like climbing a mountain. I constantly yearned for sleep, as if hoping to escape the reality that weighed me down. Mentally, I felt drained, struggling to muster the energy or motivation to envision a brighter future. Physically, I was a shadow of my potential, with the mere thought of running a single mile seeming like an insurmountable challenge.

Doubts clouded my mind. Was I destined to be this perpetual underachiever? The societal label of a 'loser' haunted my thoughts, making me question if I'd ever secure a stable full-time job or achieve any semblance of success. The narrative I told myself was one of inevitable failure, and it felt like I was trapped in a cycle of despair and resignation.

Turning point

One day, amidst the fog of fatigue and self-doubt, a spark of clarity emerged. The haunting image of becoming like my father, resigned to a life of cleaning floors and missed opportunities, became a stark reminder of a path I desperately wanted to avoid. I yearned for more than just a job; I wanted a career, something I could genuinely take pride in. A vocation that, when asked about, would make my chest swell with pride and my eyes light up. This newfound determination became my driving force, the catalyst propelling me towards a future I knew I deserved but had to fight for.

Discovering David Goggins' "Can't Hurt Me" was a turning point. The book's powerful message resonated deeply, guiding me towards mental and physical discipline. Goggins' journey inspired me to push beyond my limits, shaping me into the person I am today.
